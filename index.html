Week #3 -> Crowd-sourcing

I decided to explore "Exhausting a Crowd". I think the concept is very interesting. I believe that this is a way to criticise "turking". It is very clear that only humans(or a very good AI) would be able to accurately describe what is happening in the footage. And even then, the footage quality is pretty low and some actions are very hard to see. People try to note what is happening, but we quickly see that what we see can be very different for me and for other viewers. We each have our own perception on what is going on, what we think the people in the footage are feeling, doing, going to. I think this shows how biased our view on such a simple thing like camera footage is. The things we notice and the "data" we create is biased and the problem would be to assume that this data is reality and true. Now, imagining that this kind of footage could be analyzed by artificial intelligence that can make errors and that is trained on data that is biased as well, it is even more dangerous to take this as the truth. Knowing this, it would make sense to not use the conclusion of the analysis of camera footage to make decision, but unfortunately, information like that is used by governments and law enforcement. Using tools like this can be useful and good if used in the right way. But, we always need to keep in mind that the technology we use is always biased and that we need to keep this in mind when using it.
